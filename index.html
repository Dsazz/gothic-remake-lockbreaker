How to map your lock
  1. Count the locks and pick the matching number above.
  2. For each lock, set its starting hole (1 = wall, 4 = notch).
  3. Turn that lock once in-game. Each other lock that moves — mark With (same way) or Against (opposite). With/Against apply to every turn direction for that pair.
  4. Grinding means a pin hits wall 1 or 7 — not the same as an Against coupling.

Lockpicking tier (Fingers, Old Camp)

Training does not change how plates move on a legal turn — only mistake budget and (at Master) link removal after a broken pick.

Tier Wall mistakes On pick break
Untrained 2 Lock resets
Trained (10 LP + 100 ore) 4 Progress kept
Master (20 LP + 200 ore) 6 Progress kept; one link removed

At Master, if you've snapped picks on this lock, set how many under I · The Lock, then tap Gone beside each dropped coupling on the tumbler cards before re-solving.

Locks are numbered 1 (front) to N (back). Cards stack back at top, front at bottom, matching the in-game view. Hole 4 is the notch; holes 1 and 7 are walls.

Common questions
What is Gothic Remake Lock Breaker?
Gothic Remake Lock Breaker is a free browser-based lockpicking solver for Gothic 1 Remake. Map each plate's starting hole and couplings (With or Against), and it returns a step-by-step turn sequence that opens the lock.
How is it different from net-turn calculators?
Net-turn calculators only give total turns per plate. Gothic Remake Lock Breaker uses edge-safe search to find sequences that never grind a pin against wall holes 1 or 7, so your pick does not snap mid-sequence.
Is Gothic Remake Lock Breaker free?
Yes — free, no account, and runs in any modern browser including phones. Open gothiclockbreaker.com beside the game, map your lock, and follow the step-by-step walkthrough.
